Selfie death

     “A photograph one has taken of oneself, typically taken with a smartphone or webcam and shared via social media.”

     Popularly known as Selfie is a well-known form of self-photography. It is an act that boosts self-confidence in some, and to some it is a way to be seen and recognized amidst the social stigma across various standards ranging from beauty to lifestyle.

     Although it started with people clicking pictures of themselves, the obsession went a little too far with time. Even the marketing strategies of famous brands included selfies to exhibit themselves effective on social media. In 2016 alone, people uploaded 24 billion selfies online. As per the study conducted by Lee and Sung (2016), people took approximately 93 million selfies each day.

      Taking too many selfies is often considered as an mentally unhealthy sign by the medical authorities. Research says that obsession with taking selfies is usually a sign of anxiety disorder related to personal appearance known as body dysmorphic condition.

      To set themselves apart, people started clicking selfies which had substantial risks associated with them. In a study conducted at the All-India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS), Bhubaneswar, India, in April–August 2018. One in ten medical and nursing students took a dangerous selfie. One in a hundred reported being injured while attempting to take a selfie.

     According to a report published on 4 October 2018 by BBC, 259 people died while taking selfies in 2018 alone. As per a report published in the family medicine and primary care journal, the highest number of casualties due to precariously clicking selfies was recorded in India, followed by Russia, US, and Pakistan.
