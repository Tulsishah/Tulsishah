Photography

     From capturing the obvious to unlocking some of the most intricate creations of nature, photographs have indeed seen a long journey. The early ones were grainy, black and white but promising enough. It was very different from the painting because it was documenting real life, real people, and actual events in action. It felt like the time had frozen, and it was a window to the past. The entire history could now be seen through these pictures. It influenced public opinion, helped understand global events, and brought about societal change.

     "Photographic" redirects here. For the image obtained, see Photograph. For other uses, see Photography (disambiguation). Getting a portrait done of oneself used to be an upper-class affair. An average person could never think of a picture painted of themselves. Introductory photography changed this scenario once and for all. It empowered every individual in society by giving them access to photos beyond portraits. It gave a different perspective of the world by capturing the system that was never seen before. The world witnessed what it never did with its naked eye.

     The most crucial development in photography has to be the Kodak cameras. Released in 1888, this first camera was just 1 dollar. With the advent of these, people could explore photography even without professionals or the photo studios. The photo albums became a trend back then, and people clicked candid, relaxed, and natural pictures.

     Modern photography has found a home in everything one can imagine. People have classified photography into as many as 106 different types. Few include Fashion, nature, wildlife, abstract, conceptual, candid, wedding or event, Hi-speed, Infrared, Food, nude, still, life, satellite, etc.; the list is non-exhaustive. Two of the most trending forms are 'Wedding/Event' photography and 'self-portraits or selfies.' As the name suggests, pictures in the former type have to be taken by a professional or an amateur with proper gear.

     This form of photography is so popular that it has become quite a choice of profession for youngsters.

     On the particular day, couples vicariously experience and preserve their important date through professionally captured images of themselves. Not only this, people are so obsessed with self-portraits or selfies that they click pictures literally everywhere they go. May it be a brunch with friends, a night out with loved ones, an event as informal as a wedding, or as formal as a meeting, people love to click their images everywhere. Ever since the launch of the iPhone, having a compact camera, fast and convenient, the importance of taking pictures has only increased with time.

     Photography is a growing industry and has crossed 1lakh (INR) worth and is growing at the rate of 25-50% every year. In India, an average photographer earns around 3.5 lakh (INR)/year during his early career period; however, with experience, skill, and location. In India, weddings and events are lavish affairs. Thus, people are likely to spend a considerable amount on it. As per a report published in the Hill Post, around 52% of Indians are willing to pay 30k(INR) to hire professional wedding photographers. In metro cities, the price can go up to 5 lakhs (INR).

     Photographs have become an irreplaceable part of our lives. The face allows us to connect with people on virtual social platforms, support our documents, capture a time with the family, and many more. It gives us the freedom to show the world what we see and express ourselves. Photography is no longer an art. It is also a technical skill one needs to master. A photograph has a lot of technical aspects to it as well, like the knowledge of the triangular exposure, different modes as per the need, depth, shadows, highlights, etc. Nowadays, cameras are also programmed to show filters in other apps.

     Imagine hiring a professional or seeking random people every time you go out on a trip to click a family picture. It would be so not pocket-friendly and annoying. Now imagine being in an excellent location, but you neither have a phone nor a camera because either was restricted. Also, think of a solo trip, and all you ask for is a full-length picture, including the backdrop. These are a few examples of problems that do exist in real life. To take care of such issues, an innovative approach is needed. The world is turning tech-savvy, and technology has the key to addressing all these troubles we face regularly. Those days are not far when pictures could be clicked without having to ask a stranger or hire a photographer.
